Motivation

Solving nonlinear bilevel problems to global optimality is a long-standing challenge in optimization theory. Special cases of bilevel problems, such as problems of linear (quadratic) â€“ linear (quadratic) type, have been studied extensively and many algorithms have been proposed in the literature (Dempe, 2002; Floudas and Gounaris, 2009). However, the general nonconvex form is very challenging and only recently the first deterministic method was proposed by (Mitsos et al., 2008).

Why BASBL?

Branch-And-Sandwich BiLevel BASBL solver is a MINOTAUR solver for nonlinear bilevel problems. BASBL is based on a recent Branch-and-Sandwich algorithm (see References section for the theory behind him) which guarantees to solve a broad class of bilevel problems to global optimality. Moreover, BASBL is enhanced with several new techniques: reduction of overhead of the UB procedure, alternative ways to calculate tighter bounds, as well as alternative choices for branching and node selection.

Important: BASBL solver is still under active design and development and not feature complete or ready for consumption by anyone other than software developers.

Test Problems

Description of nonconvex bilevel test problems in AMPL format, compatible with BASBL v0.1 is provided on test-problems-wiki-page. This wiki provides:

  • Errata List for test problems taken from (Mitsos and Barton, 2007)
  • The formulation of the problems and brief analysis of the feasible set and how the optimal solution can be obtained.
  • Helpful illustrations of the objective functions and geometry of the problems
